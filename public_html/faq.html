What areas do you install earthquake valves?

Based in Irvine, we primarily serve the greater Orange County area. However, we travel to other areas in Southern California for an additional mileage fee. Please contact us for an estimate.

How much does it cost?

Prices vary and depend upon how many units are included, materials needed and price for requisite permits. The price for a standard installation on a single family dwelling is $279.00. We can offer a sizeable discount to associations and multiple meter complexes requesting several valves.

What is included in the price of installation?

Included is the installation of the valve, restoration services, inspecting for natural gas leaks inside and outside the building, servicing all natural gas appliances, painting the meter assembly area and demonstrating the operation of the valve.

What kind of valve is installed?

We exclusively use The Little Firefighter valves in either vertical or horizontal models. They have a 30 year warranty.

I know how to shut-off my gas, why do I need an earthquake valve?

An earthquake can occur at any moment. You may be at work, away from home, or even asleep. A quick response is crucial to preventing a fire or explosion if an earthquake cause a broken gas line. An earthquake valve automatically shuts off your gas.

How long does it take to install an earthquake valve?

On average, an standard install takes 45 minutes. The preliminary inspection will determine if the install may take longer. If we are doing several installs at the same time, total install time will be shorter.

Do you need to shut off the gas to my house during the installation process?

Yes. Your gas service will temporarily be halted during installation. However, when we turn your service back on we’ll relight the pilots on your appliances.

What insurance companies offer discounts with the earthquake valves?

Allstate, Nationwide, and several Farmers and State Farm agents offer discounts. If your agent will not give you a discount we can refer you to several that do.

How large of an earthquake does it take to trip the valve?

Valves are set to trip at a 5.4 magnitude or greater when the home is located in the epicenter.

Doesn’t my water heater strap serve the same safety purpose?

Water heater bracing can protect it from falling over, however other unknown circumstances can cause the natural gas lines to break or leak (e.g. other items falling on the gas lines, stress on the gas lines, leaks or other breaks to other natural gas appliances).

Living in Orange County, will small earthquakes be tripping the valve unnecessarily?

No. The earthquake valve is designed to safely shut off the gas supply in larger, more dangerous earthquakes which are more likely to cause serious damage from a gas leak.

Will I have to purchase and install a new earthquake valve after each 5.4+ quake?

No. These devices are fully capable of being manually reset and will be ready to protect your house again during the next major earthquake. (*always your local gas supplier after an earthquake to turn your gas supply back on and inspect your gas appliances for any leaks and safe operation).

If the valve trips during an earthquake or by accident, who do I call?

Any licensed plumber or Gas Company representative can safely restore the gas service. In working with associations, we typically train the maintenance staff how to perform the restoration.

Are there any monthly/annual fees involved with these safety valves?

Absolutely not! Our one-time fee is just that. After that, you can rest assured that this device will continue to protect your home and loved ones for years to come.

Are earthquake valves required by law?

Some cities and counties throughout Southern California do require the installation of an earthquake valve, and many more are heading in that direction. Requirements vary by local ordinance. Also, some insurance companies are now requiring an earthquake valve installed before issuing homeowner policies.
